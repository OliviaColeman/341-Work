Basic Information About Mushrooms

Mushrooms are fungi. They do not contain clorophyll, which is what plants use to manufacure food and energy. Mushrooms break down non-living organic matter as a food source. A mushroom's typical life span ranges between one day to one week, depending on the type of fungi. Sometimes they last less than 24 hours. The fungus material is called mycelium. Mycelium is made out of countless little filaments woven together. These filaments are called hyphae. The hyphae secrete the enzymes that break down the organic material. Mushrooms produce spores instead of seeds. The spores are microscopic single cells that are protected by a layer of chitin.
